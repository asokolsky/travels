# Dubrovnik, Croatia to Trieste, Italy Road Trip: Day 0 + 5 Driving Days

Assumption: rented car, one-way route, and no driving day over about 4 hours. Day 0 is a pre-road-trip Dubrovnik arrival day; Days 1-5 are the fast driving version. Rick Steves' public [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia)/[Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia) guidance says short trips should focus on priorities and not underestimate distances, so this version keeps [Dubrovnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dubrovnik), [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar), [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia), [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar), [Plitvice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park), and [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste), while making [Brela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela)/[Baska Voda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%C5%A1ka_Voda) a coastal stop instead of an overnight. [[1]](#source-1)[[2]](#source-2)

## Rick Steves Lens

- Prioritize the big-impact stops: [Dubrovnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dubrovnik), [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar), [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia), and [Plitvice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park). [[1]](#source-1)[[2]](#source-2)[[3]](#source-3)
- Use the car where it helps most: [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina), [Peljesac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelje%C5%A1ac)/[Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ston), [Plitvice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park), and the final [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia)/[Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy) approach. [[1]](#source-1)[[4]](#source-4)
- Keep old-town time unrushed: early mornings and evenings matter more than adding extra checklist stops. [[1]](#source-1)
- Treat [Plitvice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) as the major inland nature detour. In 5 days, this means skipping [Opatija](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opatija), [Piran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piran), [Postojna Cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postojna_Cave), and [Skocjan Caves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%A0kocjan_Caves). [[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)
- Skip [Hvar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hvar)/[Korcula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kor%C4%8Dula), [Krka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krka_National_Park), [Sibenik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%A0ibenik), and [Istrian hill towns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istria). [[1]](#source-1)

## Day 0: Dubrovnik, Croatia

Estimated driving: none if you are already in town. If this is arrival day with a rental car, avoid driving into the old center and confirm parking or drop-off logistics before committing to an Old Town hotel.

Map: [Day 0 Dubrovnik core walk: Pile Gate -> Walls -> Stradun -> Sponza Palace -> Rector's Palace -> Buza Bar -> Dubrovnik Cable Car](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Pile%20Gate%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia&destination=Dubrovnik%20Cable%20Car%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=walking&waypoints=Walls%20of%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia%7CStradun%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia%7CSponza%20Palace%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia%7CRector%27s%20Palace%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia%7CBuza%20Bar%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)

- Start with the [Walls of Dubrovnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walls_of_Dubrovnik) first thing in the morning or late afternoon; this is the signature Dubrovnik sight and the most crowd-sensitive part of the day. [[11]](#source-11)[[55]](#source-55)[[56]](#source-56)
- Use [Stradun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stradun_%28street%29) as the spine of the old-town walk: Pile Gate ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pile%20Gate%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)), Onofrio's Fountain ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Onofrio%27s%20Fountain%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)), the Franciscan Monastery ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Franciscan%20Monastery%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)), [Sponza Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sponza_Palace), and [Rector's Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rector%27s_Palace,_Dubrovnik). [[55]](#source-55)
- For a viewpoint, take the cable car to [Srđ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sr%C4%91) near sunset if the weather is clear and winds are calm; check the official timetable before banking on it. [[55]](#source-55)[[57]](#source-57)
- If you arrive early and want nature more than museums, swap the afternoon palace/museum time for [Lokrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lokrum). Treat it as a half-day island escape, and verify the current boat schedule because operations are seasonal and weather-dependent. [[58]](#source-58)
- End with an unhurried evening in the old town. This is the day to linger; Day 1 should leave early for [Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ston) and [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar).

Best stops:
- [Walls of Dubrovnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walls_of_Dubrovnik), especially early or late for softer light and fewer groups. [[11]](#source-11)[[56]](#source-56)
- [Stradun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stradun_%28street%29), side lanes, and harbor edges for the low-effort old-town wander. [[55]](#source-55)
- [Srđ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sr%C4%91) for the city-and-islands view, or [Lokrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lokrum) if you want swimming, shade, and a break from stone lanes. [[55]](#source-55)[[57]](#source-57)[[58]](#source-58)

Food notes:
- Buza Bar ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buza%20Bar%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) is the Rick Steves-published cliffside drink stop and works best before dinner, not as a meal plan. [[11]](#source-11)
- For dinner, Taj Mahal ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Taj%20Mahal%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) is the Bosnian/Balkan pick with Michelin Bib Gourmand recognition, Kamenice ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kamenice%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) is the simple old-town seafood/oyster option, and Kopun ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kopun%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) is better for a composed Croatian meal near the Jesuit steps. [[12]](#source-12)[[19]](#source-19)[[20]](#source-20)[[21]](#source-21)
- Reserve dinner if you care where you eat, or arrange a hosted/local meal ahead if that is more your style. Dubrovnik's old town is compact, cruise-traffic-heavy, and easy to overpay in if you improvise at peak hour. [[12]](#source-12)[[13]](#source-13)

Lodging notes:
- If you want to sleep inside the walls, Scalini Palace ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Scalini%20Palace%2C%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) gives you Old Town immediacy near [Stradun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stradun_%28street%29), but assume pedestrian luggage logistics and no simple car access. [[59]](#source-59)
- Hotel Excelsior ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Excelsior%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) is the polished seaside/Old Town splurge with easier arrival than sleeping inside the walls. [[36]](#source-36)
- Hotel More ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20More%20Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia)) in Lapad is calmer and better if you care more about parking, sea views, and bus/taxi access than waking up inside the old town. [[37]](#source-37)

Skip note: Do not try to do the walls, [Lokrum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lokrum), [Srđ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sr%C4%91), multiple museums, and a long dinner in one relaxed day. Pick the walls plus one major add-on.

## Day 1: Dubrovnik, Croatia to Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina

Estimated driving: 3.25 to 3.75 hours total, plus border time.

Map: [Day 1 route: Dubrovnik -> Ston -> Pocitelj -> Mostar](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Dubrovnik%2C%20Croatia&destination=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Ston%2C%20Croatia%7CPocitelj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)

- Leave [Dubrovnik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dubrovnik) early.
- Stop in [Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ston) and [Mali Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Ston) for the walls, salt pans, and oysters.
- Continue to [Pocitelj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Po%C4%8Ditelj) for a short walk through the old stone village.
- Sleep in [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar), ideally within walking distance of [Stari Most](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stari_Most).

Best stops:
- [Ston walls](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walls_of_Ston) and [Mali Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Ston) oysters. [[1]](#source-1)[[2]](#source-2)
- [Pocitelj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Po%C4%8Ditelj) fortress viewpoint. [[1]](#source-1)
- [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar) old town at sunset: [Stari Most](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stari_Most), bazaar lanes, and riverside views. [[1]](#source-1)

Food notes:
- Around [Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ston) / [Mali Ston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mali_Ston), make oysters the point: Kapetanova Kuca ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kapetanova%20Kuca%2C%20Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia)) is the more established seafood stop, while Bota Sare ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bota%20Sare%2C%20Mali%20Ston%2C%20Croatia)) is another classic Mali Ston oyster option. Keep this as lunch only if it does not endanger the border timing. [[22]](#source-22)[[23]](#source-23)
- In [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar), aim for Bosnian food near the bridge or Coppersmiths' Street. Good candidates are Tima-Irma ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Tima-Irma%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) for grilled meats/cevapi, Sadrvan ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Sadrvan%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) for a traditional courtyard meal, Hindin Han ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hindin%20Han%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) for riverside setting, and Harmonija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Harmonija%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) for bridge views. [[14]](#source-14)[[24]](#source-24)

Lodging notes:
- In [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar), sleep in or just above the old town so sunrise and sunset around [Stari Most](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stari_Most) are effortless. Hotel-Restaurant Kriva Cuprija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel-Restaurant%20Kriva%20Cuprija%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) is the atmospheric pick in the UNESCO old-town zone, while Hotel Almira ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Almira%2C%20Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina)) is a practical old-town base near the bridge. [[38]](#source-38)[[39]](#source-39)

Skip [Kravica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kravica_%28waterfall%29) unless the border crossing is unusually quick. [[1]](#source-1)[[9]](#source-9)

## Day 2: Mostar, Bosnia and Herzegovina to Split / Trogir, Croatia via Brela, Croatia

Estimated driving: 3.5 to 4 hours total, plus border time. Skip [Imotski](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imotski) if the border is slow. [[1]](#source-1)[[9]](#source-9)

Map: [Day 2 route: Mostar -> Blagaj -> Brela -> Split](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Mostar%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina&destination=Split%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Blagaj%2C%20Bosnia%20and%20Herzegovina%7CBrela%2C%20Croatia)

- See [Mostar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mostar) early before the day-trip crowds.
- Detour to [Blagaj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blagaj,_Mostar) for the [Buna spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vrelo_Bune) and tekke.
- Cross back into [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia).
- Stop at [Brela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela) or [Baska Voda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%C5%A1ka_Voda) for a beach walk, not a long beach day. [[6]](#source-6)
- Sleep in [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia) for nightlife and palace atmosphere, or [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir) for a quieter old-town base.

Best stops:
- [Blagaj Tekke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vrelo_Bune) and [Buna spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vrelo_Bune). [[1]](#source-1)
- [Brela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela) waterfront, [Brela Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela), and [Punta Rata Beach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela). [[6]](#source-6)
- [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia) evening walk on the [Riva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia#Riva), or [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir) if you want easier scale and parking. [[2]](#source-2)[[4]](#source-4)

Food notes:
- On the [Brela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brela) / [Baska Voda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%C5%A1ka_Voda) stop, keep food quick unless you are ahead of schedule. Restaurant Punta Rata ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Restaurant%20Punta%20Rata%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia)) is the easiest beach-adjacent choice, Tavern Galinac ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Konoba%20Galinac%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia)) is better if you want a fuller Dalmatian tavern meal, and Restaurant Plima ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Restaurant%20Plima%2C%20Brela%2C%20Croatia)) is another Brela tourism-listed option. [[25]](#source-25)
- If sleeping in [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia), choose by mood: Konoba Fetivi ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Konoba%20Fetivi%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) for traditional Dalmatian cooking, K.uzina ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=K.uzina%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) for a Michelin Bib Gourmand value meal, Dvor ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Dvor%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) for a splurge with sea-view garden setting, Buffet Fife ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buffet%20Fife%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) for a busy value-oriented local meal, or Galija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Galija%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) for casual seafood pizza/gnocchi near the promenade. [[15]](#source-15)[[26]](#source-26)[[27]](#source-27)[[28]](#source-28)
- If sleeping in [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir), Franka ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Franka%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) is the Michelin-inspected pick, while Rick Steves forum users mention Pizzeria Kristian ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pizzeria%20Kristian%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) for informal pasta/seafood and Don Dino ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Don%20Dino%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) for a more polished old-town meal. [[16]](#source-16)[[29]](#source-29)

Lodging notes:
- If sleeping in [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia), stay inside or right beside [Diocletian's Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace) only if you are comfortable with pedestrian luggage logistics and some old-town noise. Hotel Slavija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Slavija%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) is the classic in-palace practical choice, while Hotel Vestibul Palace ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Vestibul%20Palace%2C%20Split%2C%20Croatia)) is the small heritage splurge. [[40]](#source-40)[[41]](#source-41)
- If sleeping in [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir), use the old town if you want a quieter, more compact evening than Split. Hotel Monika ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Monika%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) and Heritage Hotel Tragos ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Heritage%20Hotel%20Tragos%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) fit the historic-core plan; Brown Beach House ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Brown%20Beach%20House%2C%20Trogir%2C%20Croatia)) is better if you want parking, pool time, and an easier car arrival outside the old-town lanes. [[42]](#source-42)[[43]](#source-43)[[44]](#source-44)

## Day 3: Split / Trogir, Croatia to Zadar, Croatia

Estimated driving: 2 to 2.75 hours total.

Map: [Day 3 route: Split -> Trogir -> Zadar](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Split%2C%20Croatia&destination=Zadar%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Trogir%2C%20Croatia)

- Morning in [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia): [Diocletian's Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace), the [Peristyle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocletian%27s_Palace), and [Cathedral of Saint Domnius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cathedral_of_Saint_Domnius). [[2]](#source-2)[[4]](#source-4)
- Stop in [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir) if you did not sleep there.
- Continue to [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar) for sunset.
- Sleep in [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar), preferably on or just outside the old-town peninsula for an easy evening walk to the waterfront.

Best stops:
- [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia) palace core. [[2]](#source-2)[[4]](#source-4)
- [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir) old town. [[4]](#source-4)
- [Zadar Sea Organ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_organ) and waterfront sunset. [[4]](#source-4)

Food notes:
- Public Rick Steves sources are thin on specific [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar) restaurants, so keep the evening flexible after the Sea Organ. For a polished seafood dinner, use Fosa ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Fosa%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)) or Kornat ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Kornat%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)); for a more relaxed old-town meal, look at Pet Bunara ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Pet%20Bunara%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)) or Bruschetta ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Bruschetta%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)). [[4]](#source-4)[[30]](#source-30)[[31]](#source-31)[[32]](#source-32)

Lodging notes:
- In [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar), the old-town peninsula is worth it for the [Sea Organ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_organ) evening, but parking is the catch. Almayer Art & Heritage Hotel ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Almayer%20Art%20%26%20Heritage%20Hotel%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)) is the adults-only boutique pick, Hotel Bastion ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Bastion%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)) is the heritage/spa choice near the waterfront sights, and Art Hotel Kalelarga ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Art%20Hotel%20Kalelarga%2C%20Zadar%2C%20Croatia)) is best for main-street old-town immediacy. Confirm parking before booking if you plan to keep the car overnight. [[45]](#source-45)[[46]](#source-46)[[47]](#source-47)

Rick Steves-style choice: do [Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Split,_Croatia) or [Trogir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trogir) well, not both deeply. [[1]](#source-1)[[4]](#source-4)

## Day 4: Zadar, Croatia to Plitvice / Rastoke, Croatia

Estimated driving: 2.25 to 3 hours total.

Map: [Day 4 route: Zadar -> Nin -> Rastoke -> Plitvice Lakes](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Zadar%2C%20Croatia&destination=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia&travelmode=driving&waypoints=Nin%2C%20Croatia%7CRastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia)

- Morning in [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar) old town: [Roman Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar#Architecture), [St. Donatus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_St._Donatus) area, waterfront.
- Optional quick stop in [Nin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nin,_Croatia).
- Drive inland to [Rastoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rastoke), the waterfall village near [Slunj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slunj).
- If you arrive early, use a late-afternoon [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) ticket for the Lower Lakes. [[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)
- Sleep near [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) for the earliest park start, or in [Rastoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rastoke)/[Slunj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slunj) for a prettier village setting.

Best stops:
- [Zadar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zadar) old town and [Sea Organ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_organ). [[4]](#source-4)
- [Nin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nin,_Croatia) old town, if you want a short coastal-history stop. [[4]](#source-4)
- [Rastoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rastoke) waterfalls and watermills. [[3]](#source-3)

Food notes:
- Near [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park), do dinner where you sleep rather than driving around after dark. Rick Steves forum users mention Hotel Degenija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Degenija%2C%20Plitvice%20Lakes%2C%20Croatia)), Plitvice Miric Inn ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Miric%20Inn%2C%20Croatia)), and in-park hotels as practical dinner options. If you sleep in [Rastoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rastoke) / [Slunj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slunj), ask the lodging host what is open that night; hours can be very seasonal. [[17]](#source-17)

Lodging notes:
- For the earliest [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) start, Hotel Jezero ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Jezero%2C%20Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia)) is the most convenient in-park choice near Entrance 2. Plitvice Miric Inn ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Plitvice%20Miric%20Inn%2C%20Croatia)) is a warmer family-run guesthouse option nearby, while Hotel Degenija ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Degenija%2C%20Plitvice%20Lakes%2C%20Croatia)) is a strong car-friendly choice near Entrance 1 with restaurant convenience. [[48]](#source-48)[[49]](#source-49)[[50]](#source-50)
- If you prefer the prettier village setting over maximum park convenience, Hotel Mirjana & Rastoke ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hotel%20Mirjana%20%26%20Rastoke%2C%20Slunj%2C%20Croatia)) keeps you near [Rastoke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rastoke) and works best if you are comfortable driving to the park early the next morning. [[51]](#source-51)

## Day 5: Plitvice Lakes, Croatia to Trieste, Italy

Estimated driving: about 3.75 to 4 hours direct, plus any border checks. Keep this as a direct driving day if the 4-hour limit matters.

Map: [Day 5 route: Plitvice Lakes -> Trieste](https://www.google.com/maps/dir/?api=1&origin=Plitvice%20Lakes%20National%20Park%2C%20Croatia&destination=Trieste%2C%20Italy&travelmode=driving)

- Enter [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) early. [[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)
- Recommended route in a 5-day trip: choose a shorter park route if you still need to drive to [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste) the same day. [[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)
- Drive directly to [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste).
- Finish and sleep in [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste), near the old center or waterfront if you want to walk to dinner after returning the car.
- Skip [Opatija](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opatija), [Piran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piran), [Postojna Cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postojna_Cave), and [Skocjan Caves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%A0kocjan_Caves) unless you add a 6th day. [[1]](#source-1)[[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)

Best stops:
- [Plitvice Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) boardwalks and waterfalls. [[3]](#source-3)[[5]](#source-5)
- [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste) waterfront and old center after arrival. [[1]](#source-1)

Food notes:
- In [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste), choose based on appetite after the long drive: Hostaria Malcanton ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Hostaria%20Malcanton%2C%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) for seafood and modern Triestine cooking, Buffet da Pepi ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Buffet%20da%20Pepi%2C%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) for a historic Trieste buffet experience, Osteria Bier Stube Trieste ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Osteria%20Bier%20Stube%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) for the city's local/Austrian-regional mix, or [Caffe San Marco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caff%C3%A8_San_Marco) for coffee, dessert, and old literary-cafe atmosphere. [[18]](#source-18)[[33]](#source-33)[[34]](#source-34)[[35]](#source-35)

Lodging notes:
- In [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste), prioritize the old center or waterfront after returning the car; if you still have the car overnight, verify garage or valet details before booking. Savoia Excelsior Palace ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Savoia%20Excelsior%20Palace%2C%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) is the grand waterfront choice, Urban Hotel Design ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=Urban%20Hotel%20Design%2C%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) is the smaller Cittavecchia design pick, and DoubleTree by Hilton Trieste ([map](https://www.google.com/maps/search/?api=1&query=DoubleTree%20by%20Hilton%20Trieste%2C%20Italy)) is the larger historic-center option with formal parking information. [[52]](#source-52)[[53]](#source-53)[[54]](#source-54)

## Practical Car Notes

- Confirm the rental car is allowed into [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina), [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia), and [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy), and that one-way drop-off in [Trieste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trieste) is permitted. [[9]](#source-9)
- [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina) is outside Schengen, so expect passport checks entering and leaving BiH. [[9]](#source-9)
- [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia), [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia), and [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy) are Schengen countries, but temporary internal border controls may still happen. As of May 17, 2026, the EU lists [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia) checks on land borders with [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia)/[Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary) through June 21, 2026, and [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy) checks on land borders with [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia) through June 18, 2026. [[8]](#source-8)
- [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia) requires an e-vignette for motorways and expressways. For most passenger rental cars, this is usually toll class 2A; verify the license plate before buying. [[7]](#source-7)
- [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia) uses motorway tolls, usually paid at toll booths or by card. [[10]](#source-10)
- Do not leave luggage visible in the car at beaches, old towns, or park stops.
- Book timed [Plitvice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plitvice_Lakes_National_Park) tickets ahead and sleep near the park before visiting. [[5]](#source-5)
