After doing my homework, I am arriving at some unexpected conclusions and am seeking feedback to these.

## No "married filing jointly" status

High progressive income taxes AND no "married filing jointly" status makes income equalization between the spouses beneficial.

E.g., assuming 2025 ordinary income brackets (23% for <€28k, 35% for €28k...€50k, 43% for >€50k), a single income of €100k will result in taxes of €35640 vs two incomes of €50k each result in total taxes of €28280.

## Roth IRA vs IRA vs 401(k)

Distributions from Roth IRA are taxed as ordinary income.

IRA is not recognized as a pension, while 401(k) MIGHT be.  IRA distribution is treated as ordinary income.

## Capital Gains and Dividends

Preferential treatment of capital gains (26%) and dividends (26%) for individual stocks, but not for US mutual funds.

## Conclusions

Before becoming Italian tax resident:

* Drain both spouses IRA(s) and pay US taxes, leave 401(k) intact.
* Move the money into a joint brokerage account or individual accounts which are equalized in balance.  Buy individual stocks, not US mutual funds or ETFs.

After becoming Italian tax resident:

* use dividends and long term capital gains - this should limit your tax liability to 26%.
