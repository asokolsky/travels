French *Transit Temporaire* (TT) programs let non-EU residents use a new car in
Europe for several weeks or months. The manufacturer registers and insures the
car in the traveler's name, then buys it back at the contracted return center.
Terms below were checked in July 2026.

## Programs

| Program                                                | Group         | Marques                  |
| ------------------------------------------------------ | ------------- | ------------------------ |
| [Renault Eurodrive](https://www.renaulteurodrive.com/) | Renault Group | Renault and Dacia        |
| [Car2Europe](https://www.car2europe.com/en/)           | Stellantis    | Peugeot, Citroën, and DS |

Dacia offers practical choices such as the Sandero Stepway and Duster; DS is
the premium option. No equivalent current buyback program was found for Fiat,
Opel, Jeep, Toyota, Volkswagen, BMW, Mercedes-Benz, or Volvo.

Products such as [Free2Move](https://www.opel.it/servizi-opel/mobilita-noleggio.html)
are conventional rentals or subscriptions. Brokers such as
[TT Car](https://www.ttcar.com/en/c-understand-the-buy-back-lease-483-2?force_lang=en)
arrange the same underlying manufacturer programs.

## Eligibility and coverage

Typical requirements are residence outside the EU customs territory, a
temporary European stay, a recognized driving license, age 18 or older, and no
EU-paid work. Remote workers should obtain written eligibility confirmation.
Authorized-driver rules vary and may limit drivers to qualifying close family.

The price generally includes:

- A new car with temporary registration
- Zero-deductible damage, theft, and third-party coverage
- Unlimited distance, factory warranty, and roadside assistance

Fuel, charging, tolls, parking, fines, vignettes, personal property, and winter
equipment are excluded. Check territorial limits and claim procedures; current
Peugeot and Renault terms exclude the UK.

## Italy logistics

Renault lists Rome Fiumicino, Milan Malpensa, Milan Linate, and Milan city
center. Car2Europe also serves selected Italian locations. Pickup may be free,
but a return outside France can carry a substantial fee.

Compare an Italy round trip with pickup or return in France and with a regular
Italian rental. Include travel to the depot, insurance, mileage, additional
drivers, winter equipment, and location fees.

Assumptions: 21 days in 2026, five-seat automatic compact, and pickup and return
at Rome Fiumicino or a Milan airport. US sites require an itinerary-specific
quote, so the buyback calculations use published Australian guide rates.

Budget about **$2,000** for a buyback lease after allowing for exchange-rate and
quote changes. Renault is cheapest with advance booking; otherwise Peugeot and

The Sixt range assumes €45–€70 per day for the car plus €20–€30 per day for
zero-deductible Smart or All-Inclusive Protection. Confirm unlimited mileage,
additional drivers, glass and tires, and airport fees. Basic Protection retains
a deductible and is not comparable with the buyback coverage.

Citroën's published table prices 21 days, but the same agent also states a
30-day minimum. Confirm acceptance before relying on that estimate.

All examples seat five but have limited luggage capacity. For five adults,
compare the Peugeot 2008 or 308, Citroën C3 Aircross or C4, Renault Captur, or
Dacia Duster.

## Booking cautions

- Book at least six weeks ahead; foreign delivery can take up to 42 days.
- Expect to provide a passport, license, order form, and proof of non-EU
  residence. Carry an International Driving Permit when required in Italy.
- Review cancellation charges before registration and return only to an
  approved center.
- Arrange extensions and insurance updates before the return date.
- Check winter-tire or chain laws for every destination.
