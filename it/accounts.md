For a U.S. citizen living in Italy, no single account does every job well. A
practical setup usually has four layers:

1. An Italian current account for an Italian IBAN, direct debits, and local
   payments.
1. A U.S. checking account for U.S. income, bills, cards, and ACH transfers.
1. A currency-transfer service for planned USD/EUR conversions.
1. A brokerage that explicitly accepts residents of Italy.

Provider eligibility, fees, and country policies change. Verify the exact
account type with the provider before moving or changing the legal address on
an existing account. Always give financial institutions the true residence and
tax-residency information they request.

## Italian Current Account

Use a local bank account for expenses that work best with an Italian IBAN:

- rent, utilities, condominium charges, and taxes;
- salary, pension, or other local deposits;
- SEPA transfers and recurring direct debits;
- a local debit card and access to cash; and
- local services that are operationally easier with an Italian-issued IBAN.

Compare banks on monthly fees, SEPA transfer charges, debit-card and ATM terms,
joint-account support, English-language service, branch access, and whether the
bank onboards U.S. citizens. Expect requests for a passport or identity card,
Italian tax code (`codice fiscale`), proof of address, tax-residency details,
and a U.S. taxpayer identification number.

For cash reserves, confirm which deposit-guarantee system protects the bank.
Italy's Fondo Interbancario di Tutela dei Depositi (FITD) protects eligible
deposits at member banks up to €100,000 per depositor per bank; cooperative
banks use a separate recognized scheme with similar coverage.[[1]](#source-1)

### Revolut as an Italian Current Account

Revolut can serve as the everyday Italian current account when the account is
held through Revolut Bank UAB's Italian branch and its IBAN begins with `IT`.
The Italian account supports salary deposits, SEPA transfers, direct debits,
debit-card spending, ATM withdrawals, and F24 tax payments.[[8]](#source-8)
Confirm the `IT` IBAN in the app: an older account that still has an `LT` IBAN
should not be assumed to have the same Italian-branch status or features.

The tradeoffs are that service is primarily app-based, there is no conventional
branch network, and cash deposits and cashier's checks are unavailable. ATM
allowances and other charges depend on the selected plan. Eligible deposits are
protected up to €100,000 in aggregate across the customer's deposits at Revolut
Bank UAB, but by Lithuania's deposit-guarantee system rather than Italy's FITD;
investment and cryptocurrency products are not ordinary bank deposits and may
have different protections.[[8]](#source-8)

Revolut is therefore workable as a primary transaction account, but keeping a
second Italian bank account provides redundancy and access to cash, checks, or
in-person services that Revolut does not provide.

## U.S. Bank or Credit-Union Account

Keep a U.S. account for Social Security or pension deposits, credit-card
payments, ACH transfers, and U.S. emergency cash. Confirm in writing that the
institution will continue servicing the specific account after the address is
changed to Italy. Also verify international card replacement, two-factor
authentication with a non-U.S. number, wire procedures, and beneficiary or
payable-on-death options.

### State Department Federal Credit Union

[State Department Federal Credit Union (SDFCU)](https://www.sdfcu.org/) is a
candidate because it explicitly serves members living overseas. Its membership
page lists Department of State, family or household, affiliated-organization,
and American Consumer Council paths to eligibility. It also advertises digital
access, international-transfer support, and checking-account options with ATM
or foreign-transaction-fee reimbursement.[[2]](#source-2)

Confirm the selected checking tier's current reimbursement limits and fees;
the general overseas-support language does not mean every account has identical
terms. SDFCU states that eligible deposits are federally insured by the National
Credit Union Administration to at least $250,000.[[2]](#source-2)

## Currency Transfer

A multi-currency service can be useful as a transfer rail between U.S. and
Italian accounts. [Wise](https://wise.com/) and
[Revolut](https://www.revolut.com/it-IT/) can both hold currencies, convert
USD/EUR, provide account details, and support card spending, but the legal
structure and pricing model differ.

This comparison was checked against the providers' Italy pages on July 13,
2026\.

| Consideration                     | Wise Europe                                                                                                                      | Revolut Italy                                                                                                                                                |
| --------------------------------- | -------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| Legal structure                   | Wise Europe SA is an authorized payment institution, not a bank                                                                  | The personal current account is provided by Revolut Bank UAB through its Italian branch                                                                      |
| Protection of ordinary balances   | Customer funds are safeguarded separately, but ordinary payment balances are not covered by the Belgian deposit-guarantee scheme | Eligible deposits are covered by Lithuania's deposit-insurance scheme up to €100,000 per depositor across deposits at Revolut Bank UAB                       |
| Currency conversion               | Uses the mid-market rate and shows a separate conversion fee that varies by currency, amount, and funding method                 | Uses Revolut's variable exchange rate; fees depend on plan, monthly usage, currency, and timing                                                              |
| Standard-plan pattern             | No subscription plan is required; a disclosed fee generally applies to each conversion                                           | Up to €1,000 of monthly exchange on weekdays before a 1% fair-usage fee; Standard also charges 1% for weekend exchanges under the current Italy fee schedule |
| Practical strength to investigate | One-off or recurring international transfers where the exact rate, fee, and delivered amount can be compared before confirmation | Everyday current-account and card use, especially when conversions remain within the selected plan's limits                                                  |

Wise says it uses the mid-market exchange rate and discloses a conversion fee
before the transaction; the fee varies with the currencies, amount, and payment
method.[[3]](#source-3) For an EEA resident, EUR details are provided through
Wise Europe SA. Wise safeguards customer funds separately but says it is not a
bank and its ordinary European payment balances are not covered by the Belgian
deposit-guarantee scheme.[[3]](#source-3)

Revolut's current Italian terms describe the personal account as a bank current
account with Revolut Bank UAB, operating through its Italian branch. Eligible
deposits are insured under the Lithuanian scheme up to €100,000 per depositor in
aggregate across Revolut Bank UAB deposits.[[8]](#source-8) This protection is
different from Wise's safeguarding model and does not necessarily cover
Revolut's separate investment, crypto, or other non-deposit products.

For Revolut Standard, the current Italy fee schedule provides €1,000 of monthly
currency exchange before a 1% fair-usage fee applies to additional exchange. It
also lists a 1% weekend exchange fee. Revolut uses its own variable rate, so
"no additional fee" within the allowance should not be read as a promise that
the delivered amount will always beat Wise.[[9]](#source-9) Paid-plan costs and
limits should be included when comparing a recurring conversion pattern.

For a real transfer, enter the same send amount and destination in both apps at
nearly the same time. Compare the total debit, the EUR delivered, arrival time,
funding method, and any recipient-bank charge. A headline rate or a fee-free
allowance alone is not a complete price comparison.

Operationally:

- test a small transfer before moving a large amount;
- confirm whether the route is ACH, domestic wire, SEPA, or SWIFT;
- verify the recipient name and IBAN directly in the destination account;
- keep transfer confirmations and exchange-rate records for tax basis; and
- keep enough money in each bank to avoid depending on one transfer provider
  during an account review or service interruption.

## Brokerage

The brokerage must accept the client's actual Italian residence and U.S.
citizenship. Account availability does not guarantee that every product can be
purchased. EU retail-product rules and U.S. PFIC taxation create a separate
fund-selection problem; see [PFIC vs. MiFID II](PFIC-vs-MiFID-II.html).

### Interactive Brokers

IBKR lists Italy among its available countries and describes individual, joint,
and IRA accounts, multi-currency funding, and access to markets in multiple
countries. It also notes that available currencies and features vary by the
Interactive Brokers affiliate serving the account.[[5]](#source-5)

Charles Schwab International is another possible provider because its current
application lists Italy and it serves U.S. expatriates.[[4]](#source-4) Its
disadvantages include a U.S.-dollar-centered account, country-specific product
and trading restrictions, limited cash-sweep choices for non-U.S. residents,
possible conversion or re-documentation of existing domestic accounts, and no
public guarantee that every account or asset will remain available after the
move. These limitations make it less flexible than IBKR for multi-currency cash
management and international-market access.

Before choosing a broker, obtain answers to these questions:

- Which legal entity will hold the account after Italian residency begins?
- Will the broker retain the account after a move from the U.S. to Italy, and
  which features or purchases will be restricted?
- Can an existing IRA remain at the same custodian and continue trading?
- Which cash currencies, exchanges, and transfer methods are available?
- Which U.S. and Italian tax documents or annual statements are supplied?
- How are joint ownership, beneficiaries, death, and incapacity handled?
- Which investor-protection scheme covers cash and securities, and what does it
  exclude?

### Transfer an E\*TRADE Account to IBKR

For a U.S. E\*TRADE brokerage account, the normal in-kind route is an incoming
Automated Customer Account Transfer Service (ACATS) request initiated at IBKR.
An in-kind transfer moves supported securities without selling them; the
transfer itself is therefore generally different from a taxable sale. Sales,
liquidations of unsupported assets, and later disposition remain separate tax
events.

#### 1. Match the Receiving Account

Open and fully approve the destination IBKR account before starting the
transfer. Its registration must match the E\*TRADE account:

- individual account to individual account with the same owner;
- joint account to a compatible joint account with matching owners; or
- Traditional IRA to Traditional IRA, Roth IRA to Roth IRA, and so on.

Do not transfer an IRA into a taxable brokerage account. For an IRA, confirm
that IBKR will service that IRA type after the move to Italy and use a direct
custodian-to-custodian transfer rather than taking a personal distribution.
Confirm the IBKR legal entity and account features that will apply after the
Italian address becomes effective.

#### 2. Save the E\*TRADE Records

Before submitting the request, download or record:

- the latest E\*TRADE statement showing the account title and number;
- trade confirmations and year-to-date activity;
- each position, share quantity, acquisition date, and tax-lot cost basis;
- realized gain/loss and income reports;
- cash balance, margin debit, and open orders; and
- beneficiary information and recent tax forms.

Retain these records even after the transfer. Access to the old account may
change after a full transfer, and cost-basis data can arrive separately from
the securities.

#### 3. Remove Transfer Obstacles

Let recent trades settle and cancel open orders before the transfer. Then ask
IBKR whether it accepts every position and feature in the E\*TRADE account,
especially:

- E\*TRADE or Morgan Stanley proprietary products;
- fractional shares, crypto, futures, annuities, and foreign securities;
- mutual funds that IBKR does not carry;
- options that expire soon or require permissions not enabled at IBKR;
- short positions; and
- a margin loan that exceeds IBKR's lending or collateral requirements.

Do not sell a nontransferable holding merely to make the transfer easier until
the tax effect is understood. A partial ACATS request can move supported assets
while leaving other holdings at E\*TRADE. A full request moves the complete
account and normally leads to closure of the delivering account.

#### 4. Start the Incoming Request at IBKR

In IBKR Client Portal:

1. Select **Transfer & Pay** and then **Transfer Positions**.
1. Select the intended IBKR destination account if prompted.
1. Choose **Incoming** as the transaction type.
1. Choose **United States** as the asset region.
1. Choose **ACATS** as the transfer method.
1. Select E\*TRADE from Morgan Stanley as the delivering broker and enter the
   E\*TRADE account number exactly as it appears on the statement.
1. Choose a full transfer, or choose a partial transfer and add each asset and
   quantity to move.
1. Review the account title, taxpayer information, account type, and transfer
   scope; sign and submit electronically.

IBKR's current portal instructions use this incoming U.S. ACATS path and permit
both full and partial requests.[[10]](#source-10) The request is sent
broker-to-broker; do not separately enter the same transfer at E\*TRADE unless
one of the brokers specifically instructs it.

#### 5. Fund the Transfer Fee and Monitor Status

E\*TRADE's current published schedule lists a $75 outgoing fee for a full account
transfer.[[11]](#source-11) Keep enough settled cash at E\*TRADE to cover the fee
and any debit, interest, or pending transaction. Confirm the fee directly for a
partial transfer because the public schedule quoted here specifies the full
transfer fee.

Monitor **Transfer & Pay > Transaction Status & History** in IBKR. Portal status
can progress through acknowledged, pending receipt, and available; a rejected
or held request needs correction or contact with Client Services.[[12]](#source-12)
FINRA says a properly matched, accepted ACATS transfer commonly completes in
about three to four business days, although IRAs, nontransferable assets, manual
processing, or account discrepancies can take longer.[[13]](#source-13)

Avoid trading positions involved in the transfer until both brokers show the
transfer as complete. For a full transfer, watch for later residual cash such
as dividends, interest, or settlement adjustments.

#### 6. Reconcile Positions and Cost Basis

After delivery:

1. Compare every symbol, quantity, cash balance, and margin balance with the
   saved E\*TRADE statement.
1. Confirm options, tax lots, acquisition dates, and covered/noncovered status.
1. Wait for transmitted basis data, then compare it with the saved records.
1. If tax lots remain absent or incorrect, contact both brokers. IBKR also
   provides **Performance & Reports > Tax Documents > Position Transfer** for
   entering eligible transferred tax lots.[[14]](#source-14)
1. Download the final E\*TRADE statement and retain both brokers' transfer
   confirmations.
1. Confirm that residual cash and later distributions sweep to IBKR before
   considering the old account fully resolved.

The broker's displayed basis is useful recordkeeping, but it does not replace
the taxpayer's own acquisition records. Also verify how the transferred account
and its income will be reported after Italian tax residency begins; see
[Italian Taxes](taxes.html).

Do not select a brokerage solely for access to a particular ETF. The product's
legal domicile, the client's retail or professional classification, U.S. PFIC
rules, and Italian taxation all need separate review.

## Reporting Checklist

Opening an Italian, Wise, or non-U.S.-domiciled brokerage account can create
U.S. and Italian reporting obligations even when the account earns no income.

- **FBAR:** A U.S. person generally files FinCEN Form 114 when the aggregate
  value of foreign financial accounts exceeds $10,000 at any point during the
  calendar year. The test aggregates accounts, not just the largest one, and
  does not depend on whether the accounts produced income.[[6]](#source-6)
- **Form 8938:** This is separate from FBAR. For qualifying taxpayers living
  abroad, thresholds depend on filing status and both year-end and maximum-year
  values. The IRS comparison page currently lists $200,000/$300,000 for an
  unmarried or married-filing-separately taxpayer and $400,000/$600,000 for a
  married taxpayer filing jointly.[[7]](#source-7)
- **Italian reporting:** Review foreign accounts, brokerage assets, and related
  income under `Quadro W`, IVAFE, and the applicable income schedules. See
  [Italian Taxes](taxes.html).

Keep each institution's legal name, country, account number, opening and closing
dates, highest annual balance, year-end balance, currency, statements, and
exchange-rate records. The location of the institution holding the account—not
the currency or nationality of its owner—generally determines whether it is a
foreign financial account for FBAR purposes.[[6]](#source-6)
