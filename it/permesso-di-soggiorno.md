[Permesso di Soggiorno](https://www.studiolegalemetta.com/legal-questions-and-answers/permit-to-stay-permesso-di-soggiorno/),
residence permit,
[sample](https://www.polimi.it/fileadmin/user_upload/futuri_studenti/vita-da-studente/Permesso_di_soggiorno_sample_31ago.pdf),
[instructions](https://welcomeoffice.fvg.it/media/25339/how-to-fill-out-the-forms-for-the-permit.pdf),
see also [Carta di Sogiorno](https://www.studiolegalemetta.com/legal-questions-and-answers/italian-card-to-stay-carta-di-soggiorno/).

May be obtained without becoming a [tax resident](tax-residency.html) of
Italy.

These permits are tied to the specific reason you are moving to Italy.
Common categories include:

- Lavoro subordinato (Work/Employment)
- Studio (Study)
- Motivi familiari (Family reunification)
- Residenza elettiva (Retirement / financially self-sufficient)

## Application Process

If you plan to stay in Italy for over 3 months, you must take the
[following steps](https://www.poliziadistato.it/articolo/10716):

- Arrive in Italy.
- Get a free application packet (often called the "Kit") from Post Office
  (Poste Italiane).
- Within 8 days of the arrival submit the forms back to the post
  office along with the required documents.
  - A photocopy of your passport bearing an entry visa, if required;
  - 4 recent and identical passport-size photographs;
  - A €14.62 electronic revenue stamp;
  - Documents supporting your request for the type of residence permit you are applying for.
- Visit the Police Headquarters (Questura): The post office will provide you
  with an appointment date to go to the local police department for
  fingerprinting and a formal review of your materials.

## Rights and Usage

Once issued, your permit allows you to:

- Live legally in Italy for the duration of the permit.
- Travel freely within the Schengen Area for up to 90 days out of any 180-day period.
- Access local services (like the national healthcare system, Servizio Sanitario Nazionale).

To verify the status of your application after submitting your paperwork, you
can track it using the Polizia di Stato Foreigner Portal.

## Renewal

If you are already in Italy and your residence permit is about to expire, you
must apply for renewal at least:

- 90 days before expiry date if your residence permit is valid for 2 years;
- 60 days before expiry date if your residence permit is valid for 1 year;
- 30 days before expiry date in all other cases.

The validity of your residence permit is the same as that indicated on your visa, i.e.:

- Up to 6 months for seasonal work or up to 9 months for seasonal work in the specific sectors requiring this extension;
- Up to 1 year if you are attending a duly documented course of study or a vocational training course;
- Up to 2 years for self-employment, open-ended employment and family reunification.

## Permesso di Soggiorno per motivi familiari (Family Residence Permit)

The Direct Link: Permit vs. Tax Residency

- Immigration Status: A Permesso grants you the legal right to remain in Italy for more than 90 days as the spouse of an Italian or EU citizen.
- Civil vs. Tax Residency: To get the permit, you must civilly register at your local town hall. By doing this, and by physically living in Italy for more than 183 days a year, you automatically become an Italian tax resident.
- The Rule: You generally cannot hold an Italian residence permit and maintain your primary domicile and tax home outside of the country. If you are absent from Italy for more than half the duration of your permit, it risks being revoked.
